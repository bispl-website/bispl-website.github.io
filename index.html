BISPL @ KAIST

BioImaging, Signal Processing & machine Learning Lab · Kim Jaechul Graduate School of AI, KAIST

Prof. Jong Chul Ye, IEEE Fellow

BISPL @ KAIST AI ** is a group of researchers dedicated to investigating the beauty of **bio-imaging, signal processing, and machine learning for applications across healthcare, computer vision, and scientific discovery — led by Prof. Jong Chul Ye, IEEE Fellow.

Our research is primarily focused on signal processing and machine learning for high-resolution, high-sensitivity image reconstruction from real-world biomedical imaging systems. These problems lead to investigations of fundamental questions across physics, mathematics, signal processing, biology, and medicine. We believe that actual imaging applications are a source of endless inspiration for new mathematical theories, and we are eager to solve both specific applications and application-inspired fundamental theoretical problems.

Current directions include generative models (diffusion & flow), large language models (reasoning, diffusion LMs, clinical LLMs), computer vision (text-to-image/video, 3D/4D), healthcare AI, and AI for science — building on a long history of work in computational imaging (MRI, CT/PET, microscopy) and inverse problems.
